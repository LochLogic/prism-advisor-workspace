## About / description (~1,900 chars, fits the 2,000 limit)

> Most client portals are a document vault with a login. Statements go in, clients
> rarely log in, and the actual plan — the thing the client is paying for — lives in
> the advisor's head or a PDF from last year.
>
> Prism is built on a different idea: the relationship is the product.
>
> Prism is a planning and client-experience workspace for solo and small RIAs. Every
> client gets a living lifecycle roadmap — seven phases from first paycheck to estate —
> that they can open any time, see exactly where they stand, and ask a question on
> without phone tag. Advisors run the whole relationship from the same place: planning
> tools, household ledgers, secure messaging, document workflow, meeting prep, billing,
> and a compliance-grade audit trail that's just *there* instead of being overhead.
>
> What Prism is not: another all-in-one suite asking you to rip out your stack. Your
> custodian and performance reporting are fine. Prism is the client-facing layer that
> sits on top — the part your clients actually see, finally built like it matters.
>
> Built for the practice that has outgrown the spreadsheet but isn't ready for an
> enterprise suite: white-label branding, e-signature workflow, Plaid account sync,
> calendar integration, and SEC-minded record-keeping, without an ops department or a
> five-app stack.
>
> Prism is in early access and onboarding a small group of founding design partners —
> fee-only RIAs who want a client experience worth showing off and a say in what gets
> built next. There's a fully populated live demo at prismaw.com if you'd like to click
> around first.
>
> A product of LeMay Ventures LLC.

## First-ever company post (launch announcement)

> Today we're putting a name on something we've been building quietly: **Prism**, the
> advisor workspace for solo and small RIAs.
>
> Here's the problem we kept seeing. In this business, most advice happens about four
> times a year. The other 361 days, the relationship is quiet — the client wonders
> where they stand, the advisor hopes they feel taken care of, and the "client portal"
> is a filing cabinet with a login. That quiet gap is where clients drift.
>
> Prism closes it. Every client gets a living roadmap of their financial life — seven
> phases, from first paycheck to estate — that they can open any time. They see what's
> done, what's next, and why. When something's on their mind, they ask right on the
> plan, and the advisor answers from the same workspace they run the rest of the
> practice in: planning tools, messaging, documents, billing, and an audit trail their
> compliance consultant will actually like.
>
> No rip-and-replace. Prism sits on top of the custodian and reporting stack you
> already trust — it's the client-facing layer, finally built like it matters.
>
> We're opening early access and looking for a handful of founding design partners:
> solo and small fee-only RIAs who want to help shape it (free white-glove onboarding,
> founding pricing, honest feedback both ways).
>
> There's a fully populated demo at prismaw.com — no signup, just click around. If it
> looks like your world, we'd love to talk. 👋
>
