# Version 3 | The full document (investor / serious partner)

## Prism | The advisor workspace

*A complete picture of the product, the wedge, and the business. Everything described
here is built and live unless explicitly marked as in progress.*

### 1. The opportunity

Independent and small-to-mid-size registered investment advisors (roughly 1 to 50
advisors) run their practices on a stack of disconnected tools: a planning package, a
CRM, a client portal, a performance reporter, a billing system, and a pile of
spreadsheets. They pay for all of it, integrate none of it well, and still watch clients
disengage the moment a meeting ends. The financial plan, the single most valuable thing
the advisor produces, becomes a static PDF that dies in an inbox.

This is a large, durable, fee-paying market that has been sold point solutions for two
decades and has never been sold the relationship itself as a product. That is the gap
Prism fills.

### 2. The product in one sentence

Prism is the lifecycle-planning workspace where RIAs build coordinated client plans, run
their entire book, and keep a compliance-grade record, in one place, under their own
brand, with the client as a first-class user.

### 3. The wedge: a plan that stays alive

The heart of Prism is **Wealth Horizons**, a seven-phase financial roadmap that
organizes a household's entire financial life and is advanced collaboratively by advisor
and client:

1. **Foundation** | stabilize cash flow and the household ledger
2. **Liquidity Reserve** | build the protective cash cushion
3. **Liability Optimization** | retire high-cost debt
4. **Tax-Advantaged Foundations** | maximize the HSA and sheltered space
5. **Retirement Sleeve Construction** | asset location across account types
6. **Capital Deployment** | taxable investing, harvesting, Monte Carlo
7. **Legacy and Drawdown** | Roth-conversion ladders and estate structuring

Each phase carries milestones the client can complete, flag for discussion, or open the
matching planning tool from. A flagged question lands in the advisor's inbox in real
time and is answered in a two-way thread. **This is the wedge: the plan is something the
client returns to, not a document they file away.** It is the one experience competitors
treat as a checkbox, and it is the reason clients stay engaged between reviews. Every
other capability below exists to support and surround it.

### 4. Full functionality

**Planning and analysis.** Twenty-five advisor-grade interactive tools are embedded
across the seven phases (each household's roadmap carries three to six). They span cash
flow, freedom-date and net-worth trajectory, liquidity reserve and income runway, debt
avalanche / debt-vs-invest / mortgage-payoff, HSA growth, tax-bracket headroom,
HDHP-vs-PPO, 1040 tax-return insights, asset location, contribution priority and
mega-backdoor capacity, Monte Carlo retirement projection, tax-loss harvesting,
equity-comp concentration, Roth-conversion ladder and window, withdrawal sequencing, RMD
projection, Social Security claiming-age optimization, and estate and generational
modeling. Every tool is **deterministic and explainable**: the same inputs always
produce the same output, each result traces to a line you can point at in a meeting, and
there is no black box and no score to go stale. Every analytic surface ends in a tracked
next step ("Add to agenda"), not a read-out.

**AI relationship assistant.** A guardrailed assistant (model key held server-side,
every call audited with token and latency telemetry) drafts the writing around the
practice: replies to client messages and to flagged questions, a household summary and
review talking points in the quick view, the narrative on a QBR packet, and a "who needs
attention?" triage across the whole book. A clean design line runs through it: **the AI
writes the prose, never the math.** Every number still comes from the deterministic
planning engine, and every draft is owned and approved by the advisor before it goes
anywhere. The assistant is advisor-only and never touches the client portal.

**Client experience.** A branded, installable client portal (a PWA with push
notifications) carrying the live roadmap, a real-time portfolio summary, two-way secure
messaging and in-context "discuss with your advisor" threads on any milestone, a
document vault for advisor-requested uploads and shared files, a request-a-meeting flow,
and self-serve performance and milestone reports.

**Advisor command center.** A searchable, sortable, paginated client roster with CSV
export, an intelligent alert engine (cash drag, stale relationships), a flagged-question
inbox with two-way reply threads, and a per-client workspace spanning accounts, the
meeting log, tasks, the interaction timeline, and performance.

**CRM and workflow.** Tasks with priorities, due dates, and cross-advisor assignment;
one-click review cadences; a client pipeline board (lead, onboarding, active,
review-due); prospect / proposal mode (model an unsaved household, print a proposal
packet, convert to a client in one click); calendar sync with Google and Microsoft
(tokens server-side, a week-ahead agenda on the dashboard, scheduled meetings pushed to
the connected calendar); bulk CSV client import with remembered field mappings; and a
unified interaction timeline.

**Account aggregation.** Plaid account linking and a daily balance-history time series
that powers performance and billing. (Direct custodian file and API feeds, and
holdings-level data, are on the roadmap.)

**Performance reporting.** Time-weighted return (Modified Dietz) across standard
periods, benchmark-relative comparison, an account-mix breakdown, a portfolio-value
chart, and a branded client-facing PDF.

**Billing, two engines.** SaaS subscription billing for the firm (Stripe Checkout and
webhooks), and advisory-fee billing for clients: tiered or flat fee schedules,
frequency-aware automated invoicing (monthly, quarterly, annual) driven by a scheduled
job, an approval workflow, branded invoice PDFs, and a firm revenue dashboard with
projected ARR and realized fees.

**Compliance and trust.** An append-only audit trail of every material action,
records-retained-not-erased soft deletes, immutable profile versioning, a nightly
archive of the audit trail to private storage, per-client and firm-wide exam-ready
books-and-records export over a chosen window, real DocuSign e-signature on
acknowledgements, and live multi-factor authentication, all designed around SEC Rule
17a-3 / 17a-4 record-making and retention principles. A WORM / object-lock immutable
storage tier is built and deployed, dormant until a firm configures its bucket. SOC 2
readiness is underway, and SSO / SAML is a short, pre-scoped build available for any
enterprise deal.

**Open integrations.** A public REST API (scoped, key-authenticated, per-firm), the
Zapier / Make / n8n surface on top of it, and outbound HMAC-signed webhooks for events
like a new client, a completed task, a signed acknowledgement, and an approved invoice.
Firms can wire Prism into the rest of their stack without waiting on us.

**Onboarding and growth.** A demo-first landing page (prospects explore a fully
populated workspace with no signup), true self-serve signup that provisions a firm and
its first admin automatically, client invite and claim, full white-label branding (firm
accent color, logo, and an optional "powered by Prism" across the advisor app and the
client portal), and sign-in via Google, password, or magic link.

### 5. Why it wins

- **The client side is real.** Engagement lives where the relationship lives. Clients
  use the roadmap, ask questions in context, and pull their own reports. This is the
  moat.
- **Compliance is the foundation, not a bolt-on.** Row-level security, an append-only
  audit trail, and retention were present from the first migration, not retrofitted
  under deal pressure.
- **One workspace, not five integrations.** Planning, CRM, aggregation, performance, and
  billing share one data model and one login, sitting alongside the custodian stack a
  firm already keeps.
- **Institutional feel for any size firm.** Full white-label branding of both the advisor
  app and the client portal means a solo advisor presents like a large RIA, with no
  enterprise overhead.
- **Lean, fast, and private.** A minified single-bundle app on a global edge CDN, with an
  enforced content-security policy, self-hosted libraries, and no third-party trackers.

### 6. Architecture and cost story (why the economics work)

Prism is built deliberately lean: a static single-bundle frontend on a global edge CDN,
a Postgres backend with row-level security and a thin layer of edge functions, and no
heavyweight infrastructure. Multi-tenancy and white-label branding were in the design
from the first migration, so deploying Prism across a whole network of firms is
configuration, not a rebuild. The result is an unusually low cost to serve, which means
aggressive or partner-preferred pricing is sustainable rather than a loss leader, and
the gross-margin profile is strong from the start.

benefit across many firms at once.

### 8. Status and roadmap

The product is **built and mature, not a roadmap.** A firm can use it the day it signs
in. The near-term roadmap deepens the moat rather than reaching for it: direct custodian
data feeds and holdings-level performance attribution, the custodian-direct
account-opening / paperwork flow (a planning session that recommends an action and
routes it straight into opening the account, a direct asset-gathering win for a custodian
partner), and completing the institutional trust track (SOC 2 certification, SSO on a
committed deal). The hard parts (the wedge, multi-tenancy, compliance foundations, the
integration surface) are already done.
