This is the skeleton to drop real screenshots, live design-partner names, and final numbers

## The target - why XYPN first

XY Planning Network is a membership organization for fee-only financial planners, many of
them solo or small, next-generation advisors serving Gen X and Gen Y clients. Its members are
**almost exactly Prism's ideal customer**: independent fee-only advisors who want to look
institutional without enterprise overhead. XYPN already curates and bundles a member
technology stack, so "a recommended or bundled client-facing planning layer" is a shape they
already understand.

The fit is unusually tight: we are not asking them to imagine a new buyer, we are handing
them a better version of a tool their members are already cobbling together.

---

## The core narrative

1. **The hook (ten seconds):** "Your members win clients on relationships, then lose
   engagement the moment the meeting ends. Prism is the living plan each client logs back into
   - a seven-horizon roadmap plus a two-way thread that keeps the relationship alive between
   quarterly reviews."
2. **The problem:** members stitch together a planning tool, a CRM, a portal, and a billing
   system, and their clients still treat the financial plan as a PDF that dies in an inbox.
3. **The wedge:** the client-facing roadmap and collaboration thread - the one thing only
   Prism does well, shown live, not described.
4. **The consolidation:** planning, CRM, light aggregation, performance, and billing in one
   workspace, sitting *alongside* the custodian and performance stack the firm already uses
   (so there is no losing comparison to Orion or eMoney).
5. **The institutional feel:** full white-label branding means a solo member presents like a
   large RIA - which is exactly XYPN's promise to its members.
6. **The offer:** make Prism the member-preferred (or bundled) client-experience layer for
   the network.

---

## What each side gets

**XYPN members get**
- A client portal their clients actually open, with their own brand on it.
- One workspace instead of five subscriptions, at a member-preferred price.
- Compliance-grade foundations (audit trail, retention) that fit a fee-only fiduciary.
- White-glove onboarding and bulk import, so switching is low-friction.

**XYPN gets**
- A stickier, more differentiated membership (a real tool, not another discount code).
- An on-brand member benefit it can co-market.
- An optional revenue share on member subscriptions.
- A modern, security-forward partner whose ICP is identical to its own.

## Commercial options

| Model | Shape | When it fits |
|---|---|---|
| **Member-preferred pricing** | Members get a discounted seat rate (for example a founding rate vs. the $49 list); XYPN takes a referral share (for example 10 to 20%) | Lightest touch; easy first step, keeps XYPN neutral |
| **Bundled member benefit** | XYPN licenses at a wholesale per-seat (for example $20 to $30) and includes or offers Prism inside membership at a price it sets | Deeper commitment; biggest seat volume |
| **Co-built / co-branded** | A platform fee plus per-seat, with deeper integration into XYPN's onboarding and compliance support | If XYPN wants Prism woven into its member journey |

Numbers are indicative and set on willingness-to-pay signal; the structure is the point.

## Why now, why us

- The product is **built and mature**, not a roadmap - members can use it the day they sign.
- It is **white-label and multi-tenant from the first migration**, so deploying it across a
  network is configuration, not a rebuild.
- The **cost structure is lean** (built efficiently, low infrastructure cost), so
  member-preferred pricing is sustainable, not a loss leader.
- **Compliance-grade foundations** already in place; SOC 2 readiness underway (see the
  readiness track) and SSO available for any enterprise member.

---

## De-risking the bet - the pilot

Do not ask for a network-wide rollout. Ask for a **single-quarter pilot with 10 to 20
volunteer members**, white-glove onboarded, measured on:

- activation (members who fully set up a household),
- client engagement (client portal logins / messages per household),
- member satisfaction (a short NPS-style read at the end).

A good pilot result is the entire argument for a network deal, and it costs XYPN nothing but
a few willing members.

## Objection handling

| Likely objection | The answer |
|---|---|
| "We stay neutral and already curate tools." | Offer it as a member-preferred *option*, not an exclusive mandate. Prism complements the stack; it does not force a rip-and-replace. |
| "You overlap with tools we already recommend." | We lead with the client-facing wedge, not the five-category fight. We consolidate the relationship layer and sit alongside what members keep. |
| "You are one person - key-person risk." | The lean cost structure makes the business durable, not fragile. We will offer source-code escrow, the SOC 2 track is underway, and design-partner proof is on the table. |
| "You are not SOC 2 certified yet." | The readiness track is in motion; a member-preferred pilot does not require it, and we can share the plan and a bridge letter as it progresses. |
| "Switching is painful for members." | Demo-first (no signup to try), bulk CSV import, client invite/claim, and white-glove onboarding. The pilot exists precisely to prove switching is easy. |

   portal, and white-label branding.
7. **Compliance and trust** - RLS isolation, append-only audit trail, 17a-3/4-aligned

9. **Commercial options** - the three models above with indicative numbers.
10. **Proof** - design partners and what they say.
11. **The pilot** - 10 to 20 members, one quarter, the three success metrics.
12. **The ask** - a 30-minute product walkthrough and agreement on a pilot cohort.

## Altruist variant - what changes

Altruist is a custodian built for small and solo RIAs, so the fit on the *member side* is
just as tight, but the pitch frame changes because the buyer's motivation is different.

- **Reframe the value:** for a membership org the hook is "a better member benefit." For a
  custodian it is **"deepen advisor stickiness on your custody, and gather assets."** A
  planning layer that suggests the paperwork and opens the account naturally pulls assets onto
  the custodian.
- **Lead the same wedge,** but elevate the **account-opening / paperwork hook**: the planning
  session that recommends an action ("open this IRA") and routes it straight into account
  opening at the custodian (the custodian-direct adapter tier in the ROADMAP paperwork track)
  is a direct asset-gathering win for them.
- **Commercials shift** from member pricing toward an **integration/partnership or OEM
  arrangement** - or this becomes an **acquisition conversation** rather than a license, since
  a custodian may prefer to own the layer.
- **Watch-out:** Altruist builds its own software and could choose to build rather than
  partner. Protect the relationship - lead with the integration and the live wedge, be
  deliberate about how much is shown pre-agreement, and keep the right to sell direct and to
  other partners.
