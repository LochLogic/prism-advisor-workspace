# Design-Partner Outreach Kit

A turnkey kit for recruiting the first 3–5 design partners. Goal: get real advisors using Prism, in exchange for feedback + a testimonial + a logo. One real partner is worth a hundred SEO pages.

---

## Who to target (ideal partner profile)

The wedge ICP — partners who feel the pain Prism solves and can say yes fast:

- **Solo or small fee-only RIA** (1–5 advisors), often **XYPN / NAPFA** members.
- **50–150 client households** — enough to feel tool-sprawl, small enough to switch without a committee.
- Currently stitching together **spreadsheets + a document-vault "portal" + a separate CRM**.
- Values **client experience** and a modern, transparent practice (not chasing the cheapest tool).
- Tech-comfortable; willing to give 2–3 feedback calls over 6–8 weeks.

**Avoid for now:** large multi-advisor firms (long sales cycles, compliance committees), brokerage/BD reps (different regulatory model), anyone who needs custodian feeds on day one (not built yet).

---

## The offer (what they get / what you get)

**They get:**
- Free white-glove onboarding — you set up their firm, import a few households, and walk them through it live.
- Free use through the design-partner period (and a **founding-member discount** locked in after).
- A direct line to the founder and real influence over what gets built next.

**You get:**
- 2–3 short feedback sessions over the engagement.
- Permission to use their **name, firm, and a short testimonial** once they've seen value.
- Honest answers on willingness-to-pay (informs final pricing).

Keep it to **3–5 partners** — enough signal, few enough to support personally.

---

## Positioning — say this, not that

**Lead with the wedge.** Prism is the **client-facing planning & relationship layer**: a living seven-horizon roadmap each client logs into, plus two-way collaboration that keeps the relationship alive between quarterly meetings.

- ✅ "Your clients get a living roadmap instead of a folder of stale statements — and you run the whole relationship from one place."
- ✅ "It plays nice with your custodian and reporting stack — Prism is the planning and client-experience layer on top."
- ❌ Don't lead with aggregation/performance/compliance — those are *supporting* features, and you'll invite a losing comparison to Orion/Black Diamond.
- ❌ Don't overstate compliance — "designed around SEC 17a-3/17a-4 record-keeping principles," never "guaranteed compliant."

**One-liner:** *"Prism turns each client's wealth into a living roadmap they actually log into — and gives you one workspace to run the whole relationship."*

## Where to post / who to ask (in priority order)

1. **Warm network first.** Anyone you know who is, or knows, a solo/small RIA. Highest conversion by far. Ask for intros explicitly.
2. **XYPN community** (XY Planning Network member forums / Slack) — the densest concentration of the ICP.
3. **NAPFA** member community / regional study groups.
4. **The Kitces "Members" community** and Kitcesverse discussions.
5. **LinkedIn** — search "financial planner" / "fee-only" / "RIA founder"; engage genuinely before pitching.
6. **Reddit** r/CFP and r/financialplanning (read the rules; lead with value, not a pitch).
7. **Advisor Twitter/X** (#FinTwit, fee-only planners).

> Most communities ban cold selling. Show up as a builder asking for feedback ("I built X for solo RIAs, looking for a few partners to shape it"), not a vendor.

## The flow

1. **Demo link** (no signup): `prismaw.com` → "Explore the live demo." Let them click around a populated workspace first.
2. **20-min walkthrough call.** Open on the roadmap + a live collaboration thread (the wedge), not the admin screens.
3. **White-glove setup.** You provision their firm and import 2–3 real households together.
4. **Feedback cadence.** Three touchpoints: after setup, ~3 weeks in, ~6 weeks in.
5. **Ask for the testimonial + logo** once they've hit a "this is genuinely useful" moment — not before.

## Simple tracking
A one-tab spreadsheet is plenty: `Name · Firm · Source · Status (contacted / demo'd / onboarding / active / passed) · Last touch · Notes`.
