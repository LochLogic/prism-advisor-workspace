# Founder Content Starter Pack

Ready-to-edit drafts to seed the founder-led channel (Blocker 5). Voice: Cory M. Lemay, building Prism for solo & small RIAs. Goal isn't virality - it's putting a name and a point of view behind the product so design-partner outreach lands warm.

**How to use:** post 1–2 a week on LinkedIn, in your real voice (loosen my phrasing). End the wedge-y ones with a soft "DM me if you want a look." Don't hard-sell every time - ~3 value posts per 1 ask.

---

## Post 1 - Why I built it (origin / POV)
> Most "client portals" in our industry are a document vault with a login.
>
> Statements go in. Clients almost never log in. The actual plan - the thing the client is paying for - lives in the advisor's head or a PDF from last year.
>
> I kept thinking: the relationship is the product, but the software treats it like file storage.
>
> So I've been building Prism - a workspace where every client gets a *living* lifecycle roadmap they can open any time, and the advisor runs the whole relationship from the same place. Planning and the client experience in one layer, on top of the custodian/reporting stack you already have.
>
> Curious how other solo/small RIAs think about this: is your client portal something clients actually use - or a place statements go to die?

## Post 2 - The wedge (relationship between meetings)
> A reality of this business: most advice happens ~4 times a year.
>
> The other 361 days, the relationship is quiet. The client wonders where they stand; the advisor hopes they feel taken care of.
>
> That gap is where clients quietly drift.
>
> The fix isn't more meetings - it's a relationship that stays *visible* between them. A plan the client can see, and a way to ask a question on it without phone tag.
>
> That's the core of what I'm building. Not another dashboard - a living roadmap + two-way collaboration, so the relationship doesn't go dark between reviews.
>
> How do you stay present with clients between quarterly meetings?

## Post 3 - Contrarian take (you don't have to rip out your stack)
> Unpopular opinion: most advisors don't need to replace their custodian or performance reporting. Those tools are fine.
>
> What's usually broken is the *client-facing* layer - the planning and the relationship. That's the part clients see, and it's the part that's stuck in 2012.
>
> So I'm deliberately NOT trying to out-build Orion or Black Diamond. Prism is the planning + client-experience layer that sits on top of what you already run.
>
> "One more tool" is a fair worry. But the right question isn't how many tools - it's whether the client-facing one is good. Mine usually isn't. Yours?

## Post 4 - The spreadsheet truth (problem, relatable)
> Every solo RIA I talk to has the same not-so-secret system: a spreadsheet, a document portal nobody opens, and a CRM they half-use.
>
> It works… until it doesn't. No audit trail when a cell changes. No way for the client to see their own plan. "plan_final_v3_really.xlsx."
>
> There's nothing wrong with starting in Excel. The problem is it was never a system of record - and a growing, regulated practice needs one.
>
> Building toward the thing I wish existed when a practice outgrows the spreadsheet but isn't ready for an enterprise suite. What was the moment your spreadsheet stopped scaling?
