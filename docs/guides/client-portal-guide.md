# Your Prism portal | A quick tour

Welcome. Your advisor uses Prism to keep your financial plan in one place, and
this portal is your window into it. It is yours to log into anytime, not just at
review meetings. This short tour walks through what you will see and how to use
it. Nothing here needs to be done in order, and you can always message your
advisor if anything is unclear.

## Your roadmap

The heart of the portal is your roadmap: seven horizons that build on each
other, from getting the basics steady to leaving a legacy. Each one opens to
show:

- **Milestones** - the handful of steps that matter in that horizon. Tick one
  off when it is done, or flag it with a question if you want to talk it
  through. Anything you flag goes straight to your advisor.
- **A short "why"** - plain-language reasoning for the horizon, so you always
  know the point of what you are doing, not just the what.
- **Tools** - small calculators that let you see the numbers for yourself. Where
  a milestone pairs with one, you will see a "See tool below" link.

The horizon you are working on now is highlighted as **Now**. Horizons further
out are marked **Ahead** - you can still open and explore them anytime, they are
just not your focus yet. You are never locked out of looking forward.

## The summary strip

Near the top you will see four quick numbers: your **managed assets**, your
**net worth** (everything you own, minus what you owe), how many **horizons** you
have cleared, and when your **last review** was. They update as your plan does.

## Your accounts

The **Your accounts** card lists your accounts grouped by where they are held,
each with its balance and a single total. These balances are what your advisor
has on record, so they can lag your bank or custodian's website by a few days.

If something looks off - an account missing, a balance that seems wrong - use
the **Ask your advisor** button right on the card. It starts a message for you
so you do not have to explain where you saw it.

## Account paperwork details

When it is time to open an account or move money, the paperwork goes faster if a
few details are already on file: your address, employer, citizenship, a trusted
contact, and so on. If any are missing, a card near the top invites you to
**Complete details**, which opens your information panel to exactly the fields
that are still blank.

You do not have to do it all at once. If now is not the time, dismiss the card
and it will step aside. It only comes back if something actually changes.

## Talking with your advisor

The **Conversation** card is a direct, two-way thread with your advisor. Use it
for anything between meetings - a question, a life change, a quick check-in. No
question is too small, and it keeps everything in one place instead of scattered
across email and texts.

If your advisor asks you for a document, you will see the request here too;
uploading the file answers it automatically.

## Documents

The **Documents** area is your shared file drawer. Statements, your plan
documents, and disclosures your advisor shares show up here to read or download.
You can upload your own documents as well - a statement, a tax form, anything
your advisor needs.

## Your numbers

Behind every projection is your household ledger: income, spending, savings,
debts, accounts, and goals. The **Update numbers** button opens it. Keeping it
current is the single most useful thing you can do, because every tool and
projection in the portal runs off it.

If your firm uses an approval step, your changes are sent to your advisor as a
draft to review rather than applied straight away. Either way, your work is
saved as you go.

## Meetings and invoices

If a review is scheduled you will see it noted, and you can request a meeting
from the portal. Any approved invoices from your advisor also appear here, with
a copy you can download for your records.

## Install it on your phone

The portal works in any browser, but you can also add it to your phone's home
screen like an app. Once installed, you can turn on **notifications** so you hear
about a new message, a document request, or a signature without having to check.
Look for your browser's "Add to Home Screen" or "Install" option.

## A note on privacy

Your information is yours. Your advisor sees your household; other clients never
do. Sensitive identifiers like a Social Security number are stored encrypted and
shown only as the last four digits. You will never be asked for a password or
sensitive detail over email.

## If you get stuck

The fastest help is the **Conversation** thread - your advisor is on the other
end. This guide is also something your advisor can share or print for you
anytime.
