# Prism for advisors | The first 30 days

Welcome to Prism. This guide is the onboarding spine: four weeks from a fresh
login to a practice that runs its client relationships through the workspace.
Each section is short on theory and long on "click this, then this." It is
available in the app under **Help**, and as a printable PDF you can keep on
your desk or hand to a new hire.

## Week 1 | Your workspace

- **Sign in** at prismaw.com (your firm admin sends the invite). The advisor
  app lives at `/app`; your clients will use the separate, slimmer portal at
  `/portal`.
- **Brand it.** Firm admins: Firm settings, Branding. Upload the logo, set the
  accent color, and the whole workspace, including every client portal, takes
  the firm's look.
- **Tour the dashboard.** Four things matter on day one: the KPI strip (book
  totals), the client roster (search, swipe actions on mobile, CSV export),
  the alerts rail, and the flagged-questions inbox where client questions
  land in real time.
- **Set how clients address you** in your advisor profile (first name, last
  name, or honorific) - it controls every client-facing mention of you.

## Week 1 | Add your households

Three ways, fastest first:

1. **CSV import** - Dashboard, Import. Map your Wealthbox/Redtail/Orion export
   once; Prism remembers the mapping and creates households in batches.
2. **One at a time** - New client. Name, email, phase, and optionally the
   starting financials (everything else can be filled in later from the
   Numbers panel).
3. **Prospect mode** - New prospect. An unsaved sandbox household you can walk
   through the roadmap in a first meeting, then convert to a client with one
   click if they sign (or discard without a trace).

## Week 2 | The client roadmap

Every household gets the seven-phase Wealth Horizons roadmap - the thing your
clients actually log in for.

- **Milestones** are the per-phase checklists. Clients can tick them, flag
  any of them for discussion (lands in your inbox), or jump to the planning
  tool that pairs with the milestone ("See tool below").
- **Documentation gates**: some milestones lock until the right document is
  in the vault (the chip names what is needed). You can check through a gate
  as the advisor; the override is recorded in the audit log.
- **Tools** live inside each phase: cash flow, reserve, debt avalanche, tax
  bracket headroom, Monte Carlo, asset location, withdrawal sequencing, Roth
  conversion window, and more. Every verdict has an "Add to agenda" hook that
  turns it into a CRM task on your dashboard.

## Week 2 | The Numbers panel

The household ledger behind every projection: members, income, housing,
outflow, reserve, liabilities, retirement assets, brokerage, equity comp,
insurance, estate readiness, planning and tax, goals.

- Changes **autosave** and recompute every tool instantly; Undo and Revert
  all are top-right. There is also an explicit Save button.
- If your firm enables the **approval gate**, client edits arrive as drafts
  in your "Client updates to review" inbox; approve or return with a note.
- **Government IDs (SSN)** are captured per household member here, stored
  encrypted and outside the plan data. Only the last four digits display;
  a full reveal is advisor-only and every reveal is audit-logged.

## Week 3 | Invite clients to the portal

- Quick view, **Invite to portal**: a single-use claim link pairs the client
  login to the household. The portal is installable (PWA) and supports push
  notifications for new messages, document requests, and signatures.
- What clients see: their roadmap, their numbers (editable if you allow it),
  documents, messages, meetings, invoices, and their advisor's name styled
  the way you configured. They never see advisor or firm-admin surfaces.

## Week 3 | Collaboration

- **Messages**: a two-way thread per household with an AI draft assist on
  your side. Unread indicators surface on the roster.
- **Flagged questions**: anything a client flags on a milestone lands in your
  inbox with context; reply inline, resolve when done.
- **Document requests**: ask for a statement or a tax return by category; the
  client uploads into the vault and the request closes itself.
- **Meetings**: log them, schedule them, and connect Google or Microsoft
  calendar so confirmed meetings appear on your real calendar.

## Week 4 | Paperwork and compliance

- **Acknowledgements**: request a signature on any document; escalate to a
  legally-binding DocuSign envelope with one click.
- **Account paperwork** (quick view, Paperwork): preview what Prism prefills
  onto Schwab/Fidelity account-opening forms, what is gated (SSNs release at
  submission only), and export the payload.
- **The audit trail** records every mutation, automatically. Firm admins can
  filter it, export CSVs, and print the exam-ready books-and-records packet
  for a regulator in one click.

## Week 4 | Billing

Firm admins: create fee schedules (tiered bps), assign them to clients, run
quarterly billing, and approve invoices. Clients see approved invoices in the
portal. Stripe handles your own Prism subscription separately.

## Every quarter

- Print the **QBR packet** from the quick view before each review: positioning,
  performance, plan flags (concentration, first RMD, 1040 observations).
- Refresh the **household ledger** with the client (it is the Phase 01
  discipline that keeps every projection honest).
- Sweep the **alerts rail** and your **task list**; the "who needs attention"
  AI triage on the dashboard is a good Monday habit.
