# Prism planning tools | A guide for advisors

Prism carries a working set of planning tools, organized along the seven Wealth
Horizons. Each one takes the numbers already on a household's file and returns a
specific, explainable answer: a payoff date, a bracket headroom, a claiming age, a
funded percentage. This guide is the map: what each tool does, where it lives, and
how to turn a result into a tracked next step. It is available under **Help** and as
a printable PDF.

A note up front: every tool is deterministic. The same inputs always produce the
same output, and each result traces back to a line you can point at in a client
meeting. There is no black box and no stored "score" to go stale.

## How the tools are arranged

Open a client, then open a phase. Each of the seven phases carries the two to four
tools that fit that part of the journey, so the right tool is next to the work it
informs. The tools read from the **Numbers** drawer (the household ledger:
accounts, cashflows, housing, taxes, insurance, goals), so the more complete a
household's file, the sharper the answer. Nothing here needs a separate data entry
step.

Two rules shape what you see:

- **Client returns are net of advisory fees; the advisor view is gross.** The same
  projection reads slightly differently in the portal than in your workspace, by
  design.
- **The client view informs without discouraging.** An early-journey household sees
  "Building, time on your side," never "at risk." Your view stays unsoftened, so you
  get the blunt number.

## Turn a result into an agenda item

Most advanced tools carry an **Add to agenda** action (advisor-only). One click
turns a tool's verdict into a client-linked task in your CRM, with the relevant
figures dropped into the task detail: the coverage gap and estimated premium, the
high-rate balances to prioritize, the annual Roth-conversion amount and ages. The
point of every tool is to end in a trackable next step, not a read-out.

## The tools, by horizon

### 01 Foundation

- **Cashflow** maps where the month goes and surfaces the monthly surplus that
  funds everything later.
- **Freedom Date** projects years to financial independence against the household's
  number, with a "one percent more saved" lever that shows the months it buys.
- **Net-worth trajectory** projects today's saving pace forward year by year. It
  will not compound a negative net worth (digging out is linear), and it marks the
  year the line crosses zero.

### 02 Liquidity Reserve

- **Emergency reserve** sizes the target reserve against essential spending and
  shows months of runway on hand.
- **Income protection** promotes the life-coverage gap to an interactive estimate,
  with a rough term-premium figure to frame the conversation.
- **Income runway** asks how long the reserve carries essentials if income paused,
  modeling a disability benefit and its elimination period.

### 03 Liability Optimization

- **Debt avalanche** orders payoff by rate, highest first.
- **Debt vs. invest** returns a per-debt verdict: a guaranteed payoff rate against
  the expected after-tax return on investing the same dollar.
- **Mortgage-payoff accelerator** shows the time and interest that extra principal
  saves.

### 04 Tax-Advantaged Foundations

- **HSA growth** projects the triple-tax-advantaged account forward.
- **Tax-bracket headroom** locates the household in the federal brackets and shows
  the room left before the next one, the number that frames the Roth and
  contribution decisions.
- **HDHP vs. PPO** finds the break-even between a high-deductible plan (with its HSA)
  and a richer plan.
- **Tax-return insights** reads keyed 1040 lines into deterministic, line-explainable
  observations: bracket position, withholding versus total tax, the
  standard-versus-itemized call, 0% long-term-gain harvesting room, IRMAA proximity,
  QCD eligibility, and provisional income on Social Security. No upload or OCR, so
  every observation is auditable.

### 05 Retirement Sleeve Construction

- **Asset location** places real dollars in their best account type, sheltering the
  tax-inefficient assets first and counting Roth, fit to the risk allocation. The
  what-if lever moves a dollar amount between taxable, tax-deferred, and tax-free and
  re-fits the estimated drag live.
- **Contribution priority** lays out the savings waterfall (match, then HSA, then
  IRA or Roth, then the 401(k) max, then taxable), capped by each year's room, and
  flags a missed match.
- **Mega-backdoor Roth capacity** computes the after-tax 401(k) room a plan allows.

### 06 Capital Deployment

- **Tax-loss harvesting** estimates the harvestable losses in taxable positions.
- **Monte Carlo** stress-tests the retirement plan and surfaces an exact success
  percentage (a seeded, deterministic run, so it does not wander between views).
- **Equity-comp concentration** measures single-stock risk, the embedded gain, and
  the capital-gains tax to trim or fully diversify.

### 07 Legacy & Drawdown

- **Roth conversion ladder** plans conversions across several pre-RMD years.
- **Roth conversion window** sizes each conversion to the bracket headroom in the
  low-income years before RMDs begin.
- **Withdrawal sequencing** sets a tax-efficient draw order across account types,
  with after-tax value at the horizon.
- **RMD projector** models required distributions starting at 73 on the IRS Uniform
  Lifetime divisors, which makes the Roth-ladder urgency tangible.
- **Social Security claiming** compares claiming at 62, 67, and 70 on present value
  and break-even age, and writes the chosen age back into the income stream so
  retirement readiness reflects the call.
- **Estate and legacy** projects what passes on against the federal exemption.

## From tool to plan

A few tools write straight back into the plan, so a decision made in the room sticks:

- **Social Security claiming** sets the claim age and rescales the monthly benefit
  from the household's PIA on the income stream.
- The **Asset Location**, **coverage-gap**, **debt-vs-invest**, and
  **Roth-window** verdicts each offer **Add to agenda**, creating the follow-up task.
- The **QBR** and **IPS** print packets carry the plan flags these tools raise (a
  concentrated equity position, the projected first RMD), so the meeting document and
  the tools tell one story.

## Keeping the numbers honest

The federal figures the tools lean on (brackets, the estate exemption, the IRMAA
tiers, the contribution limits) live in one dated module with a review-by year. A
build guard fails the release once the calendar passes that year, so a stale tax
figure cannot ship quietly. Statutory tables that rarely move (the RMD divisors, the
Social Security factors) are kept separately, on purpose.
